About this Archive

Intention

This archive was created to amplify stories of harm and resilience. It is meant to showcase the brutality of U.S. imperialism against the natural world.
This archive is a space not only for documentation and reckoning, but also for grief.

Creator

   My name is Nicole Schwartz and I started The Living Archive of Disappearing Things in Fall of 2021. I am a white queer person living on stolen Kumeyaay Land, within the United States. Through creating this archive, I wanted to explore embodied online experiences and digital interfaces that center sensitivity and connectivity.

   While I position myself as implicated in the environmental and human harm caused by the U.S., I stand in solidarity with the communities and ecosystems most harmed by imperialist-driven climate damage. Environmental justice must involve land back to indigenous people.
